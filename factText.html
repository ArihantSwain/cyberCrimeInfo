    • What is Home Title/Deed Theft?

    Home title theft, also known as deed theft, is the process of fraudulently putting a house deed in another person's name. A thief, in this case, steals ones identity then uses it to forge a deed, making it look like they're the property owner.

    How do criminals steal your home title?

    Many thieves often sell off properties that they, though false, claim to own through forged ids and documents. They then sell off a house or apartment and pocket the profits.


    Professionals often recommend destroying, or at the very least, securing heavily important documents to avoid possible copies / replicas being made.

    What is Email and Internet Fraud?

    Email and Internet Fraud includes all email and internet cybercrime and can encompass crimes such as identity theft, phishing, and more.

    How do I protect myself from fraud?

    Keeping financial data separate and using password managers such as Dashlane, Nordpass, or even the Google Password Manager is a great way of allowing passwords to be random yet accessible.

    Who are the main perpetrators?

    Often based in call centers, many perpetrators are part of organized groups who are committed to defrauding citizens across the globe.

    What is Identity theft?

    Identity theft happens when someone uses another person's personal identifying imformation such as card numbers, social security numbers, etc, to commit fraud or other crimes.

    How can I resolve a stolen identity?

    If someone has stolen your identity you can report it to the FTC by either phone (1-877-438-4338) or at IdentityTheft.gov. It could be necessary for police reports, or faked interactions with the police.


    Beyond that, you should file a claim with identity theft insurance, notify companies of your stolen identity, and most importantly, freeze your credit/review credit reports.

    What is Cyberextortion?

    Cyberextortion is an online crime in which hackers hold your data, system, website, etc, until met to the demands of payment. It can often use software called "ransomware" or distributed denial-of-service attacks (DDos).

    How do criminals DDoS/ DoS others?

    The core principle is that attackers will overload a server and shut down access to a web property.


    This often happens through a myriad of tools such as:

    • Low and slow attack tools

    • Applicatoin layer (L7) attack tools

    • Prtocol and transport layer attack tools

    and can often temporarily take a computer off of a network.

    What are PUPs/Potentially Unwated Programs?

    Including but not limited to: spyware, adware, dialers, etc. PUPs are often downloaded with a program that the user intended to download, almost as an add-on or secret file.

    How do I protect myself from PUPs?

    Downloading from trusted websites and checking downloads thoroughly after adding them to your machine. Remove any .exe (executable) files that may be unwanted!

    Who makes these programs?

    Though possible to be made by the criminals, malware is often very easy to make and is often made by US employees working abroad.

    What is Phishing?

    The practice of sending emails to have individuals reveal information, often through fraud or coercion.

    How can I block future phishing emails?

    Though it comes down to recognizing these emails, you can try unsubscribing to unknown emails, adding multi-factor authentication to your devices, and backing up your data.


    Beyond that, if you suspect you are being phished but are uncertain, you can message the company the sender may be posing as through an official avenue, such as their website.
